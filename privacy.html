Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-

Aquilario (“we”, “us”, “our”) respects your privacy. This page explains what we collect, why we collect it, and your choices.

Who we are

Aquilario is a small software and content studio based in Mesa, AZ. Contact us at hello@aquilario.com.

What we collect

  • Lead forms (LinkedIn & site forms): name, email, job title, company, and your responses to optional questions (e.g., bottleneck).
  • Newsletter sign-ups: email address (via our site form or Substack).
  • Contact emails: whatever you send us.
  • Analytics: we use Plausible, a privacy-friendly, cookie-less analytics tool. It collects only aggregated usage data (page URLs, referrer, approximate location by country, device type, browser). No personal profiles, no cross-site tracking.

How we use information

  • Send the resource you requested (e.g., checklist, templates) and related follow-ups.
  • Operate our newsletter and share new playbooks or updates (only if you opted in).
  • Respond to messages and provide support.
  • Understand aggregate usage to improve our content and products.

Legal bases (EEA/UK)

We rely on consent (when you submit a form or sign up) and our legitimate interests (basic analytics, site security).

Sharing

We don’t sell your data. We use service providers to run Aquilario:

  • Hosting: Netlify
  • Analytics: Plausible (cookie-less)
  • Lead capture: LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms
  • Email/newsletter: Substack (if you subscribe there)
  • Payments (if/when used): Stripe

These providers process data on our behalf under their own policies and security practices.

Retention

We keep data only as long as needed for the purposes above or as required by law. You can ask us to delete your data anytime.

Your choices

  • Unsubscribe from emails via the link in any message.
  • Request access, correction, deletion, or portability by emailing hello@aquilario.com.

Children

Our content is for adults. We don’t knowingly collect data from children under 16.

Changes

We may update this policy. We’ll change the “Last updated” date and, if material, let you know in a reasonable way.
